Practice Areas

We Handle These Types of Personal Injury Cases

If you were hurt because someone acted carelessly or broke the law, you have rights. We build strong cases around these four practice areas.

Car Accidents

Whether you were hit by a distracted driver, a drunk driver, or someone who ran a red light, we pursue full compensation for medical bills, lost wages, vehicle damage, and pain and suffering. Oregon's fault-based system requires holding the at-fault driver accountable.

Workplace Injuries

When you are hurt on the job, you deserve more than just workers' compensation. If a third party's negligence contributed to your injury, or if your employer denied valid benefits, we help you pursue every available avenue of compensation.

Slip and Fall Cases

Property owners have a legal duty to maintain safe premises. If you slipped, tripped, or fell due to a hazardous condition in a store, apartment building, or public space, you may have a premises liability claim. We investigate property maintenance records and building codes to build your case.

Medical Negligence

Doctors, nurses, and hospitals are held to a high standard of care. When a misdiagnosis, surgical error, medication mistake, or birth injury causes harm, we help families seek accountability and compensation for the physical and financial toll of medical malpractice.

Past results do not guarantee future outcomes. Every case is unique and must be evaluated on its own facts.

Know Your Legal Rights After an Injury

In the aftermath of an accident, insurance adjusters and defense lawyers will try to minimize your claim. Understanding your rights protects you from unfair settlements.

Insurance Companies Are Not on Your Side

Insurance adjusters are trained to protect their company's bottom line. Do not give a recorded statement or sign any release without legal counsel. We handle all communication so you cannot be taken advantage of.

Statutes of Limitations Are Strict

Oregon law gives you a limited window to file a personal injury lawsuit. The deadline varies depending on the type of case. Missing the deadline means losing your right to compensation forever.

You Are Entitled to Full Compensation

Your claim is not limited to medical bills. You may be entitled to compensation for lost income, reduced earning capacity, pain and suffering, emotional distress, and loss of enjoyment of life.

You Have the Right to Remain Silent

You are not required to speak with the other driver's insurance company, your employer's insurer, or anyone else about the details of your accident or your injuries. Let your lawyer speak for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answering Your Concerns About Personal Injury Claims

We work on a contingency fee basis, which means you pay nothing upfront and no hourly fees. Our fee is a percentage of the compensation we recover for you. If we do not win your case, you owe us nothing. This structure ensures that everyone has access to quality legal representation regardless of their financial situation. During your free case review, we will explain our fee structure in detail so there are no surprises.

Oregon's statute of limitations for most personal injury cases is two years from the date of the injury. However, there are exceptions. Claims against government entities have much shorter deadlines, often as little as 180 days. Medical malpractice cases have different rules. If you miss the deadline, you lose your right to file a lawsuit permanently. Contact us as soon as possible so we can evaluate your timeline and make sure your claim is protected.

Because we work on contingency, you do not need any money upfront to hire us. We front the costs of investigating your case, hiring experts, and filing court documents. Those costs are recovered from your settlement or verdict at the end. If your case does not result in compensation, you do not owe us for the fees or the costs we advanced. This system was designed to give everyone equal access to the justice system, regardless of income.

The timeline varies widely depending on the complexity of your case, the severity of your injuries, and the insurance company's willingness to negotiate fairly. Some cases settle in three to six months. Others take a year or longer, especially if surgery is involved or if the case goes to trial. During your free consultation, we can give you a realistic timeline based on the specific facts of your situation.

The value of your case depends on several factors: the total amount of your medical bills, whether your injuries are permanent, how much income you have lost, the impact on your quality of life, the strength of the liability evidence, and the insurance policy limits available. Every case is unique. We cannot give you a number without reviewing your specific situation. During your free case review, we will analyze these factors and give you an honest assessment of what your case may be worth.
